...Ok But Where?

...So Glad You Asked

Canyon Gorge Trail — easy (L’Anse)

Experience this 3.9-mile out-and-back trail near L'Anse, Michigan. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 1 h 21 min to complete. This is a popular trail for hiking and running, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day. The best times to visit this trail are May through November.

Manistique Boardwalk and River Walk — easy (Manistique)

Get to know this 3.7-mile out-and-back trail near Manistique, Michigan. Generally considered an easy route, it takes an average of 1 h 4 min to complete. This is a popular trail for hiking and walking, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.

Bare Bluff Trail — moderate (Mohawk)

Explore this 3.9-mile loop trail near Mohawk, Michigan. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 1 h 54 min to complete. This is a popular trail for birding, hiking, and walking, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day.

Sugarloaf Mountain Trail — moderate (Marquette)

Check out this 1.4-mile loop trail near Marquette, Michigan. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 42 min to complete. This is a very popular area for hiking and snowshoeing, so you'll likely encounter other people while exploring. The best times to visit this trail are April through October. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.

Chapel Falls to Chapel Beach Trail — moderate (Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore)

Head out on this 6.8-mile out-and-back trail near Shingleton, Michigan. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 2 h 21 min to complete. This is a very popular area for hiking, so you'll likely encounter other people while exploring. The best times to visit this trail are April through October. You'll need to leave pups at home — dogs aren't allowed on this trail.

Piers Gorge Trail — moderate (Norway)

Head out on this 2.6-mile out-and-back trail near Norway, Michigan. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 57 min to complete. This is a popular trail for camping, fishing, and hiking, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day. The best times to visit this trail are May through October. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.

Escarpment Trail — hard (Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park)

Get to know this 8.4-mile out-and-back trail near Ontonagon, Michigan. Generally considered a challenging route, it takes an average of 4 h 9 min to complete. This is a very popular area for birding and hiking, so you'll likely encounter other people while exploring. The best times to visit this trail are March through October. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.

Iron Belle Trail — hard (Ironwood)

Enjoy this 2.3-mile out-and-back trail near Hale, Michigan. Generally considered an easy route, it takes an average of 50 min to complete. This trail is great for birding, hiking, and walking, and it's unlikely you'll encounter many other people while exploring.
